ART FACTORY

  • Mona Lisa


  • The Mona Lisa (1503–1506) is one of the world’s most recognized portraits, housed in the Louvre Museum, Paris. Leonardo da Vinci masterfully captures a woman with an enigmatic smile and a mysterious gaze. The soft sfumato technique blends shadows and light seamlessly, creating lifelike skin and depth. Her subtle expression and the dreamlike background landscape make the painting both realistic and symbolic, leaving viewers fascinated for centuries.

  • The Starry Night



  • Painted in 1889 during Van Gogh’s stay at a mental asylum in Saint-Rémy, The Starry Night shows a swirling night sky filled with bright stars above a quiet village. Van Gogh’s bold brushstrokes and vivid colors transform the night sky into a dynamic, almost dreamlike vision. The cypress tree connects earth to the heavens, adding a spiritual element. This masterpiece reflects Van Gogh’s emotions, blending turbulence with serenity.

  • Girl with a Pearl Earring



  • Often called the “Mona Lisa of the North,” this 1665 painting by Vermeer depicts a young girl wearing an exotic turban and a striking pearl earring. Unlike traditional portraits, it captures a fleeting moment — the girl turns her head with parted lips as if about to speak. Vermeer’s use of light emphasizes her luminous skin and the glowing pearl. The intimacy and mystery of her gaze captivate viewers endlessly.
